Why Choose AARC?

At AARC, we endeavor to facilitate a path to wellbeing from a holistic perspective, that would honor trust and confidentiality. Our carefully vetted team of professionals will initiate the therapeutic connection with sincerity and integrity, encouraging a dialogue that would establish rapport. One of our key goals is to increase your emotional and ego strength to resolve conflicts, validate your emotions and learn to structure a cognitive scaffolding to exit from cognitive thinking traps. Your self-esteem and your individual profile would be our map to facilitate emotional well-being and psychoeducation, to cope with conflicts beyond the therapy sessions.

It is important to note that we have the expertise, the experience, the education, and credentials, to provide the degree of individualised attention that would facilitate change.

Therapy Options

Individual therapy & counselling

For children, adolescents, and adults (In person and online)

Group Therapy

Group therapy sessions provide a safe medium whereby, in a confidential environment, clients can validate their emotional conflicts and can learn from each other coping mechanisms with empathy and social intelligence. Eventually, support groups may emerge for the more extended period ahead. It is therapeutic in itself.

Family Therapy

Family therapy focuses on the improvement of relationships among family members. It serves multiple purposes ranging from developing skills to supporting the primary client with mental health concerns, addressing current or longstanding dysfunctional familial dynamics that may be exacerbated by recent developments, causing particular difficulties for one or more members more recently, or processing collective grief or life transition.

Relationship & Marriage Therapy

Couple therapy can help you and your partner improve your relationship if you are having communication and mis communication issues. Relationship difficulties can be resolved by helping you and your partner identify feelings generated by the discord and identify the thoughts and learn the skills to diffuse emotionally loaded thoughts, understanding each other’s perspectives and allowing them to see one another with empathy and compassion.

Meet Our Team

Every member of the team at AARC has a highly specialised skill set, coming together in order to empower our clients to reduce unnecessary pain, improve quality of life and regain independence.

Dr Feriha Peracha

M.Sc. Psychology (University of Calgary, Alberta, Canada), Ph.D. Psychology (University of London, UK), MRC Psych, UK (Fellowship), Permanent Member IARLD, Vice President Lahore Mental Health Association, Vice Chair I-Care Foundation. CEO SWAaT for Pakistan

Understanding the underlying clinical pathology, with accumulated knowledge over 40 years from multi-cultural perspectives, enables assessment that guides integrative, individualized, humanistic treatment plans. Where the client's perspective takes precedence and necessary psychoeducation is provided with empathy and compassion.

Having worked at Institutes affiliated with Universities in Canada and London (UK), clinical and neuropsychological learning and practice with guidance from highly qualified experts made this journey fulfilling, challenging, and humbling. Each client deserves the focus and attention needed to understand the strengths and vulnerabilities to guide the treatment, pathways to recovery and follow-up if necessary.

Cognitive intervention and restructuring using CBT guidelines has been the preferred treatment mode. My role as CEO of SWAaT provided the conduit to establish a partnership with Dr Sara Savage (Cambridge University, UK) in developing workshops in Pakistan to inculcate critical thinking skills, social intelligence, and empathy in students in Punjab and KPK has yielded encouraging results. I would endeavour to enable workshops that would provide an experiential learning opportunity to allow the integration of different perspectives (Integrative complexity, IC) to overcome binary black-and-white thinking. IC workshops are the Gold Standard for experiential learning.

Raafia Raees Khan

M.Sc. (Clinical) MS. (Clinical & Counselling) M.Phil. (Biological sciences, Psychology, University of Cambridge, UK).

I am a clinical and counseling psychologist practicing since 2008, with a variety of clientele focusing on clinical disorders. Hajra Raza

MSc. Psychology (conversion) (University of Surrey, UK), Level 3 certification in humanistic counselling, CPCAB, UK.

I am a psychologist practising since 2022. I have always been curious about the mental underpinnings of emotions and behaviour. I changed my study path (initially media) to satisfy my innate desire to understand human conditioning as I pursued a certification in humanistic counselling and subsequently a master’s degree in psychology. I was explicitly intrigued by, and therefore focused my research on triggers of trauma memories - even when the original stressors had long been absent - and explored the notion of why brains are especially sensitive to negative stimuli. My PTSD research motivated me to become a partner in AARC in order to create a safe space to facilitate processing trauma memories to make room for positive experiences to cherish as they move forward. AARC is a space I want our clients to be able to hold where they feel safe enough to talk about the challenging memories, associated anxieties, and find agreeable guidance and support to develop better coping.

More power to anyone who is trying to become a better version of themselves with each passing day. I have 4 years of clinical experience.

Sadia Khan Niazi

MS/M.phil. Clinical and counseling psychology, Bsc.Hon (Applied psychology), Child Accelerated Trauma Treatment (UK)

I am a clinical and counseling psychologist working since 2010. I aim to help people overcome any issues pertaining to mental health. My training is in cognitive behavioral therapy, Rational emotive behavioral therapy, client-centered therapy and gestalt therapy. However, I follow integrative therapy, which is a multi-modal therapy approach which focuses on the need of the individual seeking treatment. I also have expertise in conducting psychological assessments which can help find the basic causes of an individual’s symptoms and provides a basis of developing a treatment plan that is designed to their needs. Moreover, I am certified in children’s accelerated Trauma Treatment (CATT), which is a cutting-edge technique for treatment of PTSD (Post traumatic stress disorder). This technique is also suitable for adults who have experienced distress or traumatic experiences. It is a cognitive therapeutic approach which also includes art/play methods which suit people of all ages.

My focus of therapy is to help an individual overcome their symptoms to improve daily functioning and have enhanced emotional well-being and healing.

Offering A co-working space to Clinical and Counseling Psychologists

At AARC, We also offer spaces to qualified psychologists, counsellors, therapists, psychotherapists, life coaches, and other complementary health and wellness experts, especially those who need a private conference room to discuss with and assist their clients.

Our modern facility offers a combination of permanent and hot desks, as well as access to four private conference rooms in different sizes, each room includes space for all kinds of therapy, a client waiting area, a small event area, a shared kitchen, and other necessary office amenities like high-speed wi-fi, filing cabinets and office stationery.

Significantly, AARC is a self-service environment with limited administrative and in-person support. To meet the needs of our members, we rely on technology and effective systems and procedures.

Professional Therapists will also have the facility of flexible working hours, so they can choose the time that best suits their productivity. They can enjoy more flexibility in booking times, if the rooms are booked in advance, charges can vary depending on the booking hours and timings. All these facilities in comfortable, secure and well designed rooms.
